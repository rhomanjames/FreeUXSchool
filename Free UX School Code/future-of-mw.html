about > Future of MarketWatch

Overview

As the UX Architecture lead for the brand, created a UX strategic roadmap for MarketWatch by conducting a thorough UX audit and analyzing various resarch on MarketWatch's website.

Challenge

Purpose

I was hired a Senior UX Architect as Dow Jones, which is a news company that is comprised of various brands. One of those brands is a financial news site named MarketWatch. My main priority at Dow Jones was to lead the UX Strategy and Architecture for the MarketWatch digital experience. This project was the first step of understanding the current state of the MarketWatch site from a UX perspective, which helped me identify various areas of improvement.

Scope

MarketWatch easily surpasses 100 million page views each month, so it is essential that we continue to evolve and improve to meet the users' needs which are costantly evolving based on personal preferences and competitor product offerings.


Team

I led this effort and collaborated with the Director of UX Architecture for B2C products to add an extra perspective to the work.

Research

Our main goal of this project was not to produce a definitive visual design that would be adopted, but rather to identify the areas of improvement before moving to the visual design phase. For this reason conducting thorough research and research analysis was essential to elucidating those opportunities. Our main research methods were:
  • Competitive Analysis
  • Heuristic Analysis
  • Web Analysics
  • User Interviews/Surveys


I noted a few areas of improvement for the hamburger menu...there are a ton of items and they need to be grouped into clear categories. This will help users navigate quickly and seamlessly.

Competitive Analysis

I looked at some of MarketWatch's primary competitors to get a better idea of how MarketWatch stacked up against them. After looking at CNN Money, Financial Times, Seeking Alpha and Yahoo Finance, we found various areas of improvement.
  • Strategy
    • MarketWatch struggled with placing content intentionally for the users
  • Information Architecture
    • MarketWatch didn't provide clear and relevant pathways for users.
  • Accessibility
    • Some areas of the site were not accessible to all users.

Heuristic Analysis

I conducted a heuristic analysis using Jakob Nielsen's 10 general principles for interaction design and I disovered three primary areas of improvement.
  • Aesthetic and minimalist design
    • Overall, MarketWatch's site was very cluttered and disorganized, resulting in various pain points for users.
  • Recognition vs. Recall
    • There was a general lack of intentionality with information architecture that led to user confusion.
  • Flexibility and Efficiency of Use
    • Tools like Watchlist and Alerts were not efficiently placed throughout the site.

Web Analytics

We saw a fair amout of traffic going to our articles, which could come from third party affiliates or organic searches, but when users were on our site, over 90% of them navigated to the three following areas:
  • Watchlist
  • Market Data
  • Markets
This led us to believe that even though MarketWatch is branded as a financial news site, user may actually be going to the site primarily for their tools and research information on the markets.

User Interviews

We got our hands on some data from some user interviews from 2019 and we extracted three important concepts that should be addressed.
  • Overwhelming ads
    • People frequently commented on how ads were annoying and distracting
  • Value proposition
    • MarketWatch was moving towards a subscription model, but users stated they weren't willing to pay top dollar for financial news.
  • Trust
    • Users expressed distrust with the MarketWatch site, which could be due to the amount of ads and overall aesthetic. People couldn't differentiate the legit information from the ads/spam!

Outcomes and Next Steps

By stepping back to analyze the current state of MarketWatch, we identified various areas of improvement for the future. The primary areas are:
  • Strategy
    • MarketWatch needs to establish clear brand direction so that we can begin to build the site strategy in singular direction.
    • Is MarketWatch a financial news site that offers tools, or a financial tools site that offers news? The answer to this questions determines the trajectory of the site.

  • Personalization
    • If we have over a million daily users, not everyone wants to see the same thing. We currently present the same information in the same formate to everyone.
    • We need to present personalized content that is directly relevant to our users preferences. We need to adapt to our user's individual needs.

  • Accessibility
    • The way we present our content matters. When we present too much information at once with no clear stucture, user's can easily become overwhelmed.
    • Minimal and aesthetic designs don't just look pretty, they increase accessibility for all users and make a more marketable and usable product!
The next step in this process is aligning the product roadmap with these architectural and design needs to ensure clean and efficient implementation.
