about > Free UX School

Overview

I designed and developed the site you're on right now, FreeUXSchool.org, as a passion project. I've always wanted to share what I know about UX, plus I enjoy coding my own websites. I also did all of the graphics and copywriting.

Challenge

Purpose

UX Bootcamps and colleges are charging people crazy amounts of money to learn UX. At the time of creating this, I was a Senior UX Architect at Dow Jones where I worked with brands like Wall Street Journal and MarketWatch.com -- I didn't formally take any UX course to get to this point. I just learned on YouTube...for free! So I thought, I might as well make a free "bootcamp" that anyone can do without any financial barriers!

Mini Style Guide

Scope

I know that not everyone learns the same, so I wanted to present a variety of types of content. I wanted to think about this from a sensory standpoint.
  • Watching: Some people learn from watching videos
  • Listening: Some people learn from listening to podcasts or audiobooks
  • Reading: Some people learn from reading texts
  • Doing: Some people learn from doing interactive activities like courses with guided assignments.
I wanted to create all of these learning formats...without paying $50/month for a online teaching platform (sorry Teachable!) and coding it from scratch. So...that's how we got here.

Team

I gathered these learning resources from a variety of sources...but I was the only one designing, developing and creating the content for Free UX School.

Research

I was pretty light on detailed research for this one since it was a pure passion project, but I did do a candid competitive analysis for the different types of content.
  • Listening
    • Spotify is my main listening avenue, I wanted to create a great listening experience for everyone, so I used their child company Anchor to create a podcast.
  • Watching
    • Everybody is familiar with YouTube...so I tried to mimic their video experience. But I hate the look of the embedded YouTube videos so I used Vimeo. (Yay, no ads! :) )
  • Reading
    • We've all read articles where there are a bunch of ads wedged in the text and random videos playing on the screen to try to get you to click...I didn't want to do that to you. I made sure to create a barebones article experience for minimal distractions.
  • Courses
    • I initually purchased a membership with Thinkific...an online teaching platform so I could create this site. However...it was lacking customization and the course player for the videos just looked a little...ugly tbh. I created my own with HTML / CSS and Javascript.


I call this component a 'Content Card'. I use it all over this site! It is perfect for grouping a particular type of content while giving space for a header, image, description and call-to-action. They're clean, modern and versatile!

Design

Wireframes (Figma)

  • I created a few wireframes in Figma and on my iPad 'Notes' app with my Apple Pencil. Once I found something I liked I just ran with it. Again, it's a passion project so I didn't go through the full-fledged UXR/UXA/UXD process.
