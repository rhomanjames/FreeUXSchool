Career Preparation and Portfolio Building

Overview

Getting into UX research professionally can seem difficult, but it won't be if you prepare properly. We believe you can make it as a UX researcher! Here are a few tips for career preparation and portfolio building.

Portfolio for UX research

Include a diverse range of projects: A strong UX research portfolio should include a diverse range of projects that showcase your skills and experience. This may include projects that involve different types of research methods, industries, or user groups.

Use clear and concise language: It is important to use clear and concise language when presenting the projects in your portfolio. This will help to convey the key findings and recommendations from the research in a way that is easy for others to understand.

Use visual aids to support the presentation: Visual aids, such as charts, graphs, and diagrams, can be helpful in supporting the presentation of the projects in your portfolio. Visual aids can make it easier to communicate complex information and help to engage and retain the attention of the viewer. It is important to use visual aids appropriately and ensure that they are clear, concise, and easy to understand.

Preparing for Job Interviews and Portfolio Reviews

  • 1. Practice your communication skills: UX research often requires the ability to clearly and effectively communicate your findings and recommendations to a variety of stakeholders. Practice explaining your work in a clear and concise manner, and be prepared to provide concrete examples of the impact of your research.
  • 2. Review your portfolio: Make sure that your portfolio showcases your skills and experience as a UX researcher. Include examples of your research process, the results of your research, and the impact of your work on the final product.
  • 3. Understand the company and role: Research the company and the specific role you are applying for, and be prepared to discuss how your skills and experience align with the needs of the company and the role.
  • 4. Prepare for common interview questions: Some common questions that UX researchers may be asked in an interview include:
    • Tell me about a time when you had to overcome a challenge in your research.
    • How do you prioritize your research tasks?
    • Can you describe a specific research project and the impact it had on the final product?
    • How do you ensure that your research is representative of the target audience?
  • 5. Dress appropriately: Make sure to dress appropriately for the interview.
  • 6. Have your portfolio and resume ready to speak on.
  • 7. Be punctual: Arrive on time for the interview and make sure to allow enough time for any unexpected delays.
  • 8. Be confident: While it's natural to feel nervous, try to stay calm and confident during the interview. Remember that the interviewer wants to see your skills and experience, and is not trying to trip you up.
