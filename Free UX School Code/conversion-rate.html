What is a Conversion Rate?

By: John James

Approximate read time: 5 min

Overview

Conversion rate refers to the percentage of users who complete a desired action, such as making a purchase or filling out a form, on a website or product. It is a key metric for evaluating the effectiveness of a product or service, and is a driving force for UX design.

Conversion Rate is a Driving Force for UX

Here are a few reasons why convsersion rate is so important to UX Designers:
  • It helps to inform design decisions: By analyzing the conversion rate of a product or service, designers can identify areas of the user experience that may be causing users to drop off or abandon the process. They can then use this information to inform design decisions and make improvements to the user experience that are likely to increase the conversion rate.
  • It helps to prioritize features and functionality: By understanding the impact of different features and functionality on the conversion rate, designers can prioritize the development and design of these elements. For example, if a particular feature has a high conversion rate, designers may prioritize it over other features that have a lower impact on the conversion rate.
  • It helps to optimize the user experience: By optimizing the user experience to increase the conversion rate, designers can help the product or service to achieve its business goals. This may involve redesigning key pages or flows, improving the usability and accessibility of the product, or adding new features or functionality that are likely to drive conversions.

Strategies to Optimize Conversion Rate as a UX Designer

There are several strategies that designers can use to optimize the conversion rate of a product or service:
  • Make the call to actionclear and prominent: The call to action, or the button or link that prompts the user to complete the desired action, should be clear and prominent on the page. It should be easy for the user to see and understand, and should stand out from the surrounding content.
  • Simplify the process: The process of completing the desired action should be as simple and straightforward as possible. This may involve reducing the number of steps or fields that the user needs to complete, or providing clear and concise instructions.
  • Address user objections and concerns: By anticipating and addressing the objections and concerns that users may have about completing the action, designers can increase the likelihood of conversion. This may involve providing reassurance or incentives, such as money-back guarantees or free trials.
  • Test and optimize: By conducting A/B testing and other forms of user research, designers can identify and address issues that may be causing users to drop off or abandon the process. This can involve testing different versions of the page or process, or collecting feedback from users to understand their needs and motivations.


Conclusion

Overall, conversion rate is a driving force for UX design because it helps to inform design decisions, prioritize features and functionality, and optimize the user experience. By using strategies such as making the call to action clear and prominent, simplifying the process, addressing user objections and concerns, and testing and optimizing, designers can increase the conversion rate of a product or service and help it to achieve its business goals.
